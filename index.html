COVID-19 in Malaysia in 2021

How much has COVID-19 worsened in Malaysia in 2021?

There are a lot more cases and deaths in 2020 compared to 2021. One of the main reasons to this is the Delta variant. The Delta variant is believed to be more than twice as contagious as previous variants. We can see how much worse the COVID-19 situation is in 2021 compared to 2020 with the maps below.

Who is getting COVID-19 in 2021?

Looking at the distribution of COVID-19 cases in 2021, we can see that 70.2% of them are adults. This makes sense because adults make up of most of the population in Malaysia.

Also, due to the lockdowns, school closures, and so on, children and adolescents go out a lot less. Parents of young children also would not want to risk their child getting COVID-19. Children of the elderly would also make sure that the elderly stay at home because old people have weaker immune systems.

As we can see, children are more likely to get infected compared to adolescents. This might be because children have weaker immune systems than adolescents.

How much do vaccines help in dealing with COVID-19?

After reaching around the 50% mark for percentage of population fully vaccinated, the deaths and cases start to decrease.

Since there have been no major changes to COVID-19 guidelines and protocols before the sharp decrease in COVID-19 cases in Malaysia, and the vaccines have also been proven to be effective, we can say that the main reason why the COVID-19 cases and deaths have decreased are the COVID-19 vaccines.

In the 3 graphs below, we can effectively see how the cases start to decrease after 40.7% of the population have been fully vaccinated.

The Distribution of Various COVID-19 Vaccines in Malaysia

Among all 45,627,770 doses of the vaccines administered, 50.2% of them are from Pfizer. This is not a surprise because Pfizer's COVID-19 vaccine was the first COVID-19 vaccine listed by the World Health Organisation (WHO) for emergency use. Pfizer's vaccine was also the first COVID-19 vaccine approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). Furthermore, Pfizer's COVID-19 vaccine has 91.3% efficacy against symptomatic COVID-19.
